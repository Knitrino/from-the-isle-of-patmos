## A meditation on paradise
We started our day with a writing workshop with Silas House on the topic "Sense of Place." What a perfect topic while on Patmos, where the sense of place is millennia old. It is an island of shrubs, heavily grazed by goat herds everywhere, their little neck bells syncopating the air with music. There are limited bird species because of the lack of vegetation, but we’ve seen majestic birds of prey—the Bonelli’s Eagle and Eleonora’s Falcon. The steep hills and small cliffs plummet at every edge into the sea. And everywhere are the infinite, unnameable shades of blue. It is a sacred place, a place to be still and feel the stillness for one moment, and then the next be assaulted with the bustle of cars, buses, and flocks of people as the cruise ship dumps them off in hordes.

In the evening, we were invited to a special, private dinner organized by a celebrity friend of Silas. We sat above the beach and revered Petra Rock. It was stunning.

Unfortunately for us sisters, the host had the idea that we would sit at small tables and laid out a rule: not to sit with people you knew! This made the evening far less enjoyable for both of us by far, but we were served a meal to remember. Every bite was amazing, and the magical evening was one we will not forget!
