## A little history
Patmos was formed by a volcanic eruption during the period of transition between the Miocene and Pliocene epochs, just as mammals were also completing their biological evolution all over the Earth. When you conjure an image of this island's creation from tectonic shifts and volcanic uplift, it is exactly as you imagine: bits of island running everywhere from up high on the peak, down to the sea.

It is very much shaped like an amoeba, with "pseudopod" arms reaching out everywhere.
In Greek mythology, Artemis convinced Zeus to raise Patmos from the sea floor, and it does seem to be a piece of such beautiful rock that only the gods could have conjured.
Because of its interesting shape and small size, you can leave one stunning seaside town, go up a hill, meander the turns in the roads, and find yourself stunned again by the almost-omnipresent blue of the Mediterranean Sea.
